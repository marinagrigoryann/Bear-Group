We top-notch specialists and organize business employment training sessions, along with providing comprehensive advertising support for product promotion. Annually, our highly skilled Bear Group specialists arrange business meetings to connect with partners and showcase our products. We eagerly anticipate the opportunity to collaborate with you and extend a warm Armenian welcome. Bear Group's motto is "BRING GOOD, COMFORT, AND BEAUTY TO YOUR HOME!"

Washing interior paint


Short review


Product description:

A silky-matt emulsion paint for heavy-duty walls with very good cleanability and a huge variety of colors for your own interior design ideas. Particularly wear-resistant paint suitable for walls in all living and working areas with high surface loads - for dirt-resistant and clean walls.

Package dimensions:

4 kg, 7 kg, 14 kg, 20 kg.

Destination:

cleans up very well, a huge selection of shades.

Properties:

water-based, environmentally friendly, excellent coverage on edges, high wear resistance Wet abrasion class 2 (DIN EN 13300).

Color tones/tinting:

Base 1: white Base 1 can be applied as white paint or hand-tinted in one of the many light shades using colorants, as well as by machine.

Gloss level:

silky matte.

Tool/temperature/application:

Stir thoroughly before use, apply the paint in two layers. Dilute the paint for the first layer with no more than 10% water, apply the paint for the second layer undiluted or, if necessary, dilute with a maximum of 5% water. Application method: brush or roller. Easy to apply. Do not apply at substrate and air temperatures below +5°C. Wash tools with soap and water after use.

Dry time:

at a temperature of +20°C and relative humidity of 65%, the surface dries in 1-3 hours; after 6-8 hours, the next coat of paint can be applied. At lower temperatures and higher humidity, the drying time is longer.

Expense:

14 kg is sufficient for approximately 90 m² per coat on a smooth surface with normal absorbency. On rough surfaces, the consumption increases accordingly.

Coating structure/thinning:

Intermediate coat: Bear cleaning interior diluted with a maximum of 10% (by volume) of water. Finishing coat: not diluted washing interior, and if necessary, dilute with a maximum of 5% of water. Prime with Bear Primer if necessary.

Composition:

polyvinyl acetate ethylene dispersion, water, mineral fillers and pigment, additives. According to European regulations, the maximum permissible concentration of volatile organic compounds (VOC) in this product for the EU countries (cat. A / a): 30 g / l. This product contains less than 1 g/l VOC.

Suitable substrates and their preparation


Suitable backgrounds:

Mineral substrates: lime-cement plasters, gypsum plasters, drywall, sand-lime bricks, ceramic brick, concrete Old coatings with good bearing capacity: dispersion matt putties, plasters and paints, structural wallpapers.

Preparing the background:

Remove shabby flakes and deposits of dirt without residue with pressure jets of water or dry mechanically. The substrate after cleaning must be strong, clean, with good load-bearing capacity and free from separating agents.

Lime-cement plasters:

Do not process new plasters for 4 weeks. The plastered areas should harden well and dry. Coarsely porous, absorbent and slightly absorbent plasters should be primed with Bear Primer. Highly absorbent and slightly sandy plasters should be primed with Bear Primer twice (wet-on-wet method).

Gypsum and dispersion putties, drywall:

putty the joints, sand the sintering places, remove dust. Always prime with Bear Primer after sanding. Highly absorbent gypsum substrates should be primed with Bear Primer diluted 1:2 with water.

Ceramic or cladding:

Masonry is suitable for painting only from frost-resistant facing stone or clinker without foreign inclusions. Brickwork must be free of cracks at the seams, dry and without efflorescence. Apply the material Bear cleaning interior, diluted with a maximum of 10% (by volume) of water.

Coating with varnishes, enamels and dispersion paints with good bearing capacity:

Smoking glossy surfaces and enamel coatings. Clean soiled, chalked and old coatings with high-pressure water jets. Apply a pre-primer coat with Bear Cleansing Interior Paint diluted with a maximum of 5% water or prime with Bear Primer.

Poor bearing coatings, mineral paints:

remove without residue by sanding, brushing, scraping with high pressure water jets or other suitable means. If wet cleaning, allow the substrate to dry thoroughly before further processing. Priming should be done with Bear Primer.

Mold, nicotine, grease, soot, water stains:

Surfaces with mold and fungi should be mechanically cleaned with a damp brush, scraper or scraper. Primer coat with Bear Anti-Mildew Primer. Finish - Bear Kitchen and Bathroom. Wash nicotine, water stains, grease and soot with a fat-dissolving detergent, allow to dry well. Apply insulating paint to the solvent base.

Substrates with defects:

small defects (up to 3 mm) puttied with finishing putty from Bear. Repair large defects with a repair solution. Prime the puttied areas with Bear Primer.


Additional information


Precautions:

Keep out of the reach of children. Work must be carried out with sufficient ventilation. When working with the product, do not take food and drink, do not buy. In case of contact with skin or eyes, rinse immediately with plenty of water. Do not allow to enter sewers, soil, water. Cleaning of tools: Wash with soap and water immediately after use. When applying by spray, avoid inhalation of vapors and aerosols.

Waste management:

Unused liquid residues of the material should be taken to paint and varnish waste collection points. Dispose of dry residues and packaging as household waste.

Storage:

Store in a tightly closed package in a cool place at a temperature of at least +5°C. Guaranteed shelf life: 24 months in unopened original packaging.

Betonkontakt


Short review


Description:

Provides reliable adhesion on low absorbency substrates with quartz sand and preparation for finishing.

Package dimensions:

4 kg, 7 kg, 14 kg, 20 kg.

Destination:

Ready-to-use mineral-filled water-dispersion primer to create an adhesive rough surface for exterior and interior use. The product is intended for application on dense, smooth, non-absorbent or slightly absorbent substrates, concrete in order to create an intermediate adhesive (cohesive) layer before applying mineral plasters, reinforcing and adhesive compositions, putties, structural plasters.

Properties:

high adhesion to mineral substrates, concrete and water-dispersion coatings; water-borne; highly environmentally friendly, low odor; has a pink tint for reliable control of substrate priming; high vapor permeability (class V1 according to EN 1062).

Color tones/tinting

The product is produced in pastel pink for reliable control of substrate priming. Not subject to additional coloring.

Gloss level:

matte.

Application method/tool:

Stir the primer thoroughly before use. Apply grooving in 1 layer. The primer is applied with a roller or brush. To ensure the required characteristics, the primer must be applied at a rate of at least 6-7m²/kg.

Drying time/application temperature:

Apply at a substrate, air and product temperature of at least +5°C. At a temperature of +20°C and relative humidity of 65%, it dries on the surface in 1-3 hours. Further processing of the coating is possible after complete drying of 6-10 hours. At lower temperatures and higher humidity, the drying time increases.

Expense:

14 kg is enough for approximately 85m² per coat on a smooth surface with normal absorbency. On rough surfaces, consumption increases. The exact consumption is determined by the tailor's application.

Coating structure/thinning:

Primer coat: Highly porous, highly absorbent, loose substrates should be primed with Bear ConcreteContact Deep Penetration Primers. Adhesive coating: apply Bear ConcreteContact in 1 layer. The primer is applied without dilution at a flow rate of at least 6-7m²/kg.

Composition:

dispersion of acrylic copolymer, ox, mineral fillers and pigment, additives. The maximum allowable concentration of volatile organic compounds (VOC) in this product for the EU countries (Cat. A / a): 30g / l. This product contains less than max. 1 g/l VOC.

Suitable substrates and their preparation


Suitable base types:

lime-cement, dispersion, silicate, gypsum plasters and putties, concrete, silicate and facing bricks, old matt dispersion paints and plasters, old matt alkyd and oil paints, matt facing tiles (indoors).

Preparing the background:

Remove moss, algae, decayed flakes and dirt deposits without residue with high-pressure water jets or dry mechanically. The substrate after cleaning must be strong, clean, with good bearing capacity and free from substances that prevent adhesion (eg fats, oils, resins, etc.).

Lime-cement plasters:

New lime-cement plasters are recommended not to be treated for 4 weeks. The plastered areas should harden well and dry. Apply Bear ConcreteContact on normally and slightly absorbent plasters. Highly absorbent and lightly crumbling plasters should be primed with Bear ConcreteContact twice (wet-on-wet method).

Concrete:

Concrete substrates with deposits of dirt or scree should be cleaned mechanically or with high-pressure water jets. On normal concrete substrates, apply Bear ConcreteContact without pre-treatment. Chalking or highly absorbent concrete substrates must first be primed with a deep penetration primer Bear Concrete Contact.

Ceramic or cladding (indoors only):

Matt masonry without foreign inclusions is suitable for priming. The masonry must be without cracks at the seams, dry and without efflorescence. The primer coat should be made with Bear ConcreteContact paint without dilution.

Coating with varnishes, enamels and dispersion paints with good bearing capacity:

Glossy surfaces and enamel coatings should be matted and dust-free. Clogged, chalky old coatings should be cleaned with high-pressure water jets. Apply a primer coating Bear ConcreteContact.

Poor bearing coatings, mineral paints:

remove without residue by sanding, brushing, scraping, high pressure water jets or other suitable means. If wet cleaning, allow the substrate to dry thoroughly before further processing. Preliminary priming should be done with a deep penetration primer. Then apply a primer coating Bear ConcreteContact.

Surfaces with efflorescence:

Remove efflorescence dry with a brush. Apply a preliminary primer coat with a special solvent-based deep penetration primer. When applying coatings on surfaces with efflorescence, long-term adhesion cannot be guaranteed due to the possibility of repeated efflorescence, which impairs adhesion in the primer and under the paint layer.

Substrates with defects:

Small defects (up to 3 mm) should be puttied with facade putties, for interior work use Bear Finishing Putty. Repair large defects with a repair solution. Filled areas should be primed with Bear ConcreteContact.

Additional information


Precautions/tool cleaning:

Keep out of the reach of children. Work must be carried out with sufficient ventilation. Do not eat, drink or smoke while handling the product. In case of contact with skin or eyes, rinse immediately with plenty of water. Do not allow to enter sewers, soil, water. During application of the product, avoid contact with the surrounding surfaces. Rinse splashes immediately from lacquered, glass, ceramic, metal and natural stone surfaces.

Waste management:

Unused liquid residues of the material should be taken to paint and varnish waste collection points. Dispose of dry residues and packaging as household waste.

Storage:

Store in a tightly closed container in a cool place at a temperature of at least +5°C. Warranty period: in unopened original packaging 24 months from the date of manufacture.

Deep Penetration Primer Concentrate


Short review


Description:

Deep penetrating acrylic concentrate for leveling the moisture absorption of mineral substrates for outdoor and indoor use from the Bear product group of materials.

Package dimensions:

5, 10 l.

Destination:

concentrate for grounding the primer for painting with dispersion paints for indoor and outdoor use. For strengthening substrates and equalizing the absorbency of coarsely porous, highly absorbent and slightly sandy plasters and mineral surfaces.

Properties:

good penetrating ability; strengthens and reduces absorbency; water-borne; environmentally friendly; easy to apply; enhances the adhesion of subsequent layers of paint.

Color tones/tinting

is transparent.

Gloss level:

silky-matte after drying.

Instrument/Temperature:

Stir the primer thoroughly. Apply with a brush, roller or spray gun. Do not apply at air and substrate temperatures below +5°C. Wash tools with water immediately after use.

Dry time:

At +20°C and 65% relative humidity dries on the surface after about 2 hours and is resistant to rain, ready for further processing after about 5 hours. At lower temperatures and higher humidity, the drying time increases accordingly.

Expense:

approximately 7 m²/l (approx. 30 ml concentrate or 150 ml after dilution 1:4 per 1 m²) on slightly absorbent substrates. On a highly absorbent substrate, correspondingly more. The exact consumption is determined by trial application.

Coating structure/thinning:

Depending on the absorbency, apply after dilution 1-2 times. Do not use undiluted. In the normal case, dilute 1:4, in the case of highly and very absorbent substrates (eg gypsum plaster) dilute the product in a ratio of 1:5 to 1:9 (1 part of the concentrate with the appropriate parts of water) and mix well. For highly absorbent substrates, dilute 1:5. For priming slightly absorbent substrates dilute 1:3. When priming, do not allow the formation of gloss on the substrate after drying.

Composition:

Synthetic resin dispersion, pigments, mineral fillers, water, additives. European norms for the content of volatile organic substances (VOC) for products of this category A/h: 50 g/l (2007) / 30 g/l (2010). This product contains less than 7 g/l VOC.

Suitable substrates and their preparation


Suitable backgrounds:

mineral substrates: lime-cement plasters; gypsum plasters; silicate brick; ceramic brick; concrete.

Preparing the background:

Remove moss, algae, decayed flakes and dirt deposits without residue with high-pressure water jets or dry mechanically. The substrate after cleaning must be strong, clean, with good load-bearing capacity and free from separating agents.

Lime-cement plasters:

Do not process new plasters for 2-4 weeks. The plastered areas should harden well and dry. Coarsely porous, absorbent and slightly absorbent plasters should be primed with Bear primer. Highly absorbent and slightly sandy plasters should be primed with Bear Primer.

Ceramic or cladding:

Masonry is suitable for painting only from frost-resistant facing stone or clinker without foreign inclusions. Brickwork must be free of cracks at the seams, dry and without efflorescence. The primer coat should be done with Bear primer materials.

Concrete:

Concrete substrates with deposits of dirt or scree of sand should be cleaned mechanically or with high-pressure water jets. On slightly absorbent or suitably smooth substrates, prime with Bear. Prime normal or highly absorbent substrates with Bear Primer.

Poor bearing coatings, mineral paints:

remove without residue by sanding, brushing, scraping, high pressure water jets or other suitable means. If wet cleaning, allow the substrate to dry thoroughly before further processing. Priming should be done with Bear materials.

Surfaces with efflorescence:

not suitable for use on surfaces with efflorescence.

Substrates with defects:

Fill small defects with Bear material. Large defects up to 20 mm should be repaired with Bear material. Filled areas should be primed with Bear primer materials.

Additional information


Safety instructions:

Keep out of the reach of children. In case of contact with eyes, rinse immediately with water. Do not inhale "mist" when spraying. Do not allow to enter sewers, waterways and soil.

Waste management:

Dispose of hardened paint residues and empty containers as household waste.

Storage:

Store in a cool, dry, frost-free place and protected from direct sunlight. Shelf life in original unopened packaging is at least 2 years.
